# Industry Selection and Prioritization — Complete Analysis

## How to Read This Document

Every BizBuySell industry (114 total) has been evaluated against four filters:

1. **Data Quality** — Is the BizBuySell data credible and based on sufficient transaction volume?
2. **Search Intent Quality** — Do owners of these businesses actually search for valuation data?
3. **SERP Penetrability** — Can a new site rank for these terms within 18 months?
4. **Content Differentiation** — Can 400+ words of genuinely unique content be written per industry?

Each industry is rated HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW on each filter, then assigned to one of four tiers. **The tiers are not a publishing schedule.** All pages that pass the quality gates publish simultaneously on launch day — the sandbox clock runs from day one regardless of page count, and topical completeness is strongest when the full structure launches together.

**The tiers define data readiness priority:** which industries have the clearest data, the richest content differentiation, and the strongest search demand — and therefore which industries the data research work should focus on first when completing `industry-multiples.json`.

**Tier 1 — Research first.** All four filters HIGH or HIGH/MEDIUM. Highest compound revenue potential. Complete these JSON entries before any others.  
**Tier 2 — Research alongside Tier 1.** Three filters HIGH, one MEDIUM. Strong pages worth building. Complete these before considering Tier 3.  
**Tier 3 — Research if capacity allows.** Mixed ratings. Lower priority — pages will be built but content differentiation is thinner.  
**Tier 4 — Do not build.** One or more filters LOW. These industries will not have pages in this site regardless of timeline.

## TIER 4 — Do Not Build

These industries fail one or more filters decisively. Building pages for them would produce thin content that cannot rank and may harm the domain's quality signals.

**Truck Stops** — Median $5,250,000. This is not a Main Street business. Buyers are institutional. No individual owner searches "truck stop valuation."

**Casinos** — Regulatory complexity, institutional buyers, no meaningful independent market.

**Banking and Loan Businesses** — $1,500,000 median, institutional buyers, regulated beyond Main Street.

**Check Cashing Businesses** — 1.82× multiple, declining industry, regulatory pressure.

**Magazines and Newspapers** — Declining sector, institutional buyers, no meaningful independent owner base.

**Production Companies** — Too broad and too specialized simultaneously. Buyers are strategic.

**Dollar Stores** — 1.39× is the lowest meaningful multiple in the dataset. Dominated by franchise/chain; independent dollar stores are not actively sold.

**Energy and Petroleum Production** — Industrial, regulatory, institutional.

**All Manufacturing categories with median > $1M** — These are middle-market transactions: Chemical Mfg ($1.29M), Industrial Machinery ($1.17M), Lumber ($1.2M), Metal Products ($1.2M), Rubber/Plastic ($1.4M). Buyers are private equity and strategic acquirers. The typical Main Street BizBuySell seller is not in these categories.

**Delivery/Freight Routes** — 1.78× multiple, very low median ($120,000), route-based businesses have specialized valuation (multiples of weekly revenue, not SDE). Would require a completely different calculator logic.

**Durable/Nondurable Goods Wholesale** — B2B, complex deal structures, specialized buyers.

## The Working Number

**Tier 1: 32 industries** — research first, highest compound revenue potential  
**Tier 2: 28 industries** — research alongside Tier 1  
**Tier 3: 25 industries** — research if capacity allows, lower content differentiation ceiling  
**Tier 4: ~29 industries** — permanently excluded

**Total launch target: all Tier 1 + Tier 2 industries (60) plus any Tier 3 industries with complete data.** Every page with complete JSON data publishes simultaneously on launch day. No page publishes before every page is ready. No page publishes with incomplete `deal_notes` or generic content fields regardless of which tier it is in.

The data research work follows the tier order — Tier 1 first, then Tier 2 — but the publishing decision is binary: everything ready, or nothing live.

## Data Observations Worth Noting for Content Strategy

**The surprise multiples** — these are pages where the calculator result will genuinely shock users (in a positive way), driving shares and return visits:
- Car Washes: 4.99× (nearly 5× SDE — most owners expect 2-3×)
- Funeral Homes: 4.28×
- Laundromats: 3.65× (most owners think laundromats are low-multiple businesses)
- Medical Billing: 3.71×
- Marinas: 3.93×

These pages should be written with the "reveal" in mind — the H1 and opening paragraph should acknowledge the common misconception before presenting the actual data.

**The low multiple categories** — these pages require different framing. The user's valuation will be lower than they hoped. These pages are still worth building but the content must be empathetic and forward-looking:
- Food Trucks: 1.71× (below what most food truck owners expect)
- Nail Salons: 1.76×
- Delivery Routes: 1.78× (excluded, but noted)
- Dollar Stores: 1.39× (excluded)
- Hair Salons: 2.04× (on the lower side — manage expectations)

**The categories where the multiple varies most widely** — these pages benefit most from the What-If slider because the range between low and high quartile is large:
- Car Washes (multiple can range from ~3× to ~7× depending on membership model and format)
- Funeral Homes (pre-need revenue dramatically changes multiple)
- Insurance Agencies (book retention post-sale drives 1.2× to 2× range)
- Dental Practices (DSO vs individual buyer changes structure entirely)
